## 1.0 INTRODUCTION

### 1.1 Background of the Study
The transportation sector, particularly bus travel, is a critical component of public transport in East Africa. However, many bus companies still rely on manual booking systems where passengers must visit physical offices to purchase tickets. This process is time-consuming, prone to errors, and inefficient. The International Bus Booking System (IBBS) aims to digitize this process, offering a seamless online platform for "Wema Travellers" to manage bookings, routes, and fleet operations.

### 1.2 Problem Statement
Current manual booking systems face several challenges:
1.  **Inconvenience:** Passengers must travel to booking offices, leading to long queues and wasted time.
2.  **Data Loss:** Paper-based records are easily lost or damaged.
3.  **Lack of Transparency:** It is difficult for management to track real-time revenue and seat occupancy.
4.  **Inefficiency:** Manual ticketing is slow and requires excessive paperwork.

### 1.3 Objectives
**General Objective:** To design and implement a web-based bus booking system that automates ticket reservations and fleet management.

**Specific Objectives:**
1.  To develop a user-friendly interface for passengers to search routes and book seats online.
2.  To create an administrative dashboard for managing buses, routes, and staff (agents).
3.  To implement a secure reporting module for tracking revenue and bus occupancy.
4.  To generate digital tickets with QR codes for easy verification.

### 1.4 Scope of the Study
The system will cover:
*   **User Module:** Registration, Login, Booking, Ticket Viewing.
*   **Admin Module:** Fleet Management, Route Scheduling, User Management, Reporting.
*   **Agent Module:** Booking on behalf of customers.
*   **Geographical Scope:** Focus on routes within East Africa (e.g., Nairobi, Kampala, Dar es Salaam).

### 1.5 Significance of the Study
*   **For Passengers:** Convenience of booking from anywhere, anytime.
*   **For Management:** Improved operational efficiency and data-driven decision making.
*   **For Researchers:** A reference for future studies on transport automation.
